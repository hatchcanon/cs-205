Final Project

Introduciton

One of the most frustrating situations to find yourself in is a course where all of your friends are in the "easier section". For most of us, it feels like this happens all of the time. As part of a growing set of GPA visualizations, students in CS 205: Data Driven Discovery took the deepest dive into the GPA data to date to find out whether the perception of different sections is true or not.

Using GPA data from the most recent six semesters (Fall 2014 through Fall 2016), we found the distribution of every section/instructor group within every course. Every such group is visualized and displayed against the course average.

Legend

The distribution of final grades for a course is displayed as a stacked distribution.

   
The lightest region shades grades recieved by only top/bottom 5% of students (10% total)
Slightly darker regions shows grades recieved by students between 5% and 10% (10% total)
Even darker regions shows grades recieved by students between 10% and 25% (30% total)
The darkest regions shows grades recieved by middle 50% of students (50% total)
